# Common Customer Scenarios

## Overview

Customer support representatives handle a wide variety of customer inquiries every day. This document outlines common scenarios, recommended troubleshooting steps, and professional response examples.

---

# Scenario 1 — Wrong Product

## Description

The customer received an item different from what was ordered.

### Verification

Confirm:

- Customer Name
- Order Number
- Product Ordered
- Product Received

### Resolution Process

1. Verify the order details.
2. Confirm the incorrect item received.
3. Apologize for the inconvenience.
4. Arrange a replacement or refund according to company policy.
5. Provide return instructions if required.

### Example Response

> I sincerely apologize for the mistake. I've confirmed the order details, and we'll arrange a replacement as quickly as possible. I'll also provide instructions for returning the incorrect item if needed.

---

# Scenario 2 — Damaged Product

## Description

The customer reports that the delivered product arrived damaged.

### Verification

Request:

- Order Number
- Photos of the damaged product
- Description of the damage

### Resolution Process

1. Verify the purchase.
2. Review the submitted evidence.
3. Confirm eligibility under the company's return or warranty policy.
4. Process a replacement or refund.
5. Inform the customer about the next steps.

### Example Response

> I'm sorry your product arrived in this condition. Once we review the provided photos, we'll proceed with the appropriate replacement or refund according to our policy.

---

# Scenario 3 — Late Delivery

## Description

The customer's shipment has not arrived within the expected delivery time.

### Verification

Confirm:

- Order Number
- Shipping Address
- Tracking Number

### Resolution Process

1. Check the shipment status.
2. Identify the reason for the delay.
3. Provide the latest available update.
4. Share the estimated delivery date if available.
5. Offer compensation if company policy allows.

### Example Response

> I apologize for the delay. I've checked your shipment, and it's currently in transit. The latest estimated delivery date is within the next two business days.

---

# Scenario 4 — Missing Order

## Description

The customer reports that the order was marked as delivered but has not been received.

### Verification

Confirm:

- Order Number
- Delivery Address
- Tracking Information

### Resolution Process

1. Review the delivery status.
2. Verify the shipping address.
3. Ask the customer to check with neighbors or the building reception if appropriate.
4. Contact the shipping carrier if necessary.
5. Escalate the case if the package cannot be located.

### Example Response

> I understand your concern. I'll review the delivery information and investigate the shipment so we can determine the next appropriate step.

---

# Scenario 5 — Billing Issue

## Description

The customer reports an incorrect charge or payment problem.

### Verification

Confirm:

- Customer Name
- Invoice Number
- Payment Method
- Transaction Date

### Resolution Process

1. Review the billing records.
2. Verify the transaction.
3. Explain the billing details.
4. Correct any confirmed errors.
5. Escalate complex payment issues when necessary.

### Example Response

> Thank you for bringing this to our attention. I'll review the billing information and explain the transaction details before determining the appropriate resolution.

---

# Scenario 6 — Account Problem

## Description

The customer cannot access their account or reports account-related issues.

### Verification

Confirm:

- Registered Email Address
- Username
- Customer ID
- Identity Verification

### Resolution Process

1. Verify customer identity.
2. Check the account status.
3. Reset credentials if appropriate.
4. Unlock the account if permitted.
5. Escalate security-related issues when necessary.

### Example Response

> I'll first verify your account information for security purposes. Once verified, I'll review the account status and help restore your access.

---

# General Best Practices

When handling any customer scenario:

- Listen carefully.
- Verify customer information.
- Show empathy.
- Explain each step clearly.
- Follow company policies.
- Document every interaction.
- Escalate when necessary.
- Confirm that the customer understands the resolution.

# Key Takeaways

Successful customer support depends on:

- Professional communication
- Active listening
- Accurate verification
- Structured problem solving
- Clear documentation
- Following company procedures
- Customer-focused service

Applying these practices consistently helps improve customer satisfaction, reduce resolution time, and build long-term customer trust.
